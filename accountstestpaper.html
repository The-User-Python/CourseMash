Accounts Test Paper

Introduction

This test paper is designed to assess your knowledge of accounting principles, concepts, and practices. It consists of multiple-choice questions and short-answer questions. The total duration of the test is two hours. NOTE: Our website will not provide any kind of practical sums/question's . For practical sum's you must have to practice it by you're own only. Please do not blame the site for not providing practical sums/question's. Because it's against terms.

Instructions

1. Read the instructions carefully before attempting the test.

2. Answer all the questions in the given time.

3. Use a black or blue pen to write your answers.

4. Do not write anything on the question paper.

Sample Questions

1. What is the difference between a debit and a credit?

2. What is the purpose of a trial balance?

3. What is the difference between cash basis and accrual basis accounting?

4. What is the difference between cash book and bank statement?

5.Explain the process of recording a purchase return in the books of accounts.

6.What is a contra entry? Explain with examples.

7.Explain the difference between capital expenditure and revenue expenditure.

8.What is the difference between a provision and a reserve?

9.How does the perpetual inventory system differ from the periodic inventory system, and what are the advantages and disadvantages of each system?

10.Define the accounting equation and explain how transactions affect it.

11.What is the difference between a current liability and a long-term liability?

12.What is the difference between a prepaid expense and a deferred revenue, and how are they recorded in the financial statements?

13.How does a company account for bad debts, and what is the difference between the allowance method and the direct write-off method?

14.How are inventory costs determined under the different cost flow assumptions, such as FIFO, LIFO, and weighted average?

15.What is the difference between a classified and an unclassified balance sheet, and what are the major sections of a classified balance sheet?

16.What is the purpose of the statement of cash flows, and how are operating, investing, and financing activities reported in this statement?

17.What is the difference between a trading securities and a available-for-sale securities, and how are they reported on the balance sheet and the income statement?

18.Explain the accounting treatment for intangible assets such as patents, copyrights, and trademarks.

Scoring

The test consists of 100 marks in total. Each correct answer is worth one mark, and there is no negative marking for incorrect answers.

Conclusion

We hope this test paper will help you assess your accounting knowledge and identify areas for improvement. Good luck!
