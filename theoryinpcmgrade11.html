Theory Part of PCM for Grade 11

What is the Theory Part of Maths?

The theory part of maths involves the study of mathematical concepts, principles, and theorems, as well as their applications in various fields. It includes topics such as algebra, geometry, trigonometry, calculus, and statistics. Understanding the theory part of maths is important for developing problem-solving skills and for pursuing further studies in mathematics or related fields.

Why is the Theory Part of Maths Important?

The theory part of maths provides the foundation for understanding and solving mathematical problems. It also helps in developing logical thinking, analytical skills, and problem-solving abilities, which are useful in many different areas of life. In addition, a strong understanding of the theory part of maths is necessary for pursuing higher education in fields such as engineering, physics, computer science, and economics.

What Topics are Covered in the Theory Part of Maths?

The theory part of maths covers a wide range of topics, including:

  • Algebra
  • Geometry
  • Trigonometry
  • Calculus
  • Statistics

Theory Part of Physics for Grade 11

Chapter 1 - Physical World

Introduction to Physics, Scope and excitement, Physics in relation to science, society and technology, Need for measurement: Units of measurement; systems of units; SI units, Fundamental and derived units. Length, mass and time measurements; accuracy and precision of measuring instruments; errors in measurement; significant figures.

Chapter 2 - Units and Measurements

System of units, SI units, Fundamental and derived units. Length, mass and time measurements; accuracy and precision of measuring instruments; errors in measurement; significant figures. Dimensions of physical quantities, dimensional analysis and its applications.

Chapter 3 - Motion in a Straight Line

Frame of reference, Motion in a straight line: Position-time graph, speed and velocity. Uniform and non-uniform motion, average speed and instantaneous velocity. Uniformly accelerated motion, velocity-time, position-time graphs, relations for uniformly accelerated motion. Projectile motion.

Chapter 4 - Motion in a Plane

Scalar and vector quantities; Position and displacement vectors, general vectors and their notations; equality of vectors, multiplication of vectors by a real number; addition and subtraction of vectors, relative velocity, Unit vector; Resolution of a vector in a plane, rectangular components. Scalar and Vector product of vectors. Motion in a plane, cases of uniform velocity and uniform acceleration-projectile motion, uniform circular motion.

Chapter 5 - Laws of Motion

Aristotle’s fallacy; The law of inertia; Newton’s first law of motion; Newton’s second law of motion; Newton’s third law of motion; Conservation of momentum; Equilibrium of concurrent forces. Static and Kinetic friction, laws of friction, rolling friction, lubrication.

Theory Part of Chemistry - Grade 11

Theory Part of Chemistry - Grade 11

Chapter 1: Some Basic Concepts of Chemistry

Chemistry is the study of matter, its properties, and the changes it undergoes. This chapter introduces some of the basic concepts of chemistry such as the nature of matter, laws of chemical combinations, mole concept, empirical and molecular formulae, stoichiometry, and volumetric analysis.

Chapter 2: Structure of Atom

Atoms are the building blocks of matter. In this chapter, we study the various models proposed for the structure of atom, the discovery of subatomic particles, their properties, and arrangement in an atom. The chapter also covers the quantum mechanical model of atom, the electronic configuration of atoms and periodic classification of elements based on electronic configuration.

Chapter 3: Classification of Elements and Periodicity in Properties

The properties of elements are periodic functions of their atomic numbers. This chapter covers the periodic classification of elements based on their electronic configuration and the periodic trends in their physical and chemical properties. The chapter also introduces the concept of valency and the formation of chemical bonds.

Chapter 4: Chemical Bonding and Molecular Structure

Chemical bonds are the forces that hold atoms together in molecules and compounds. This chapter covers the various types of chemical bonds such as ionic, covalent, and metallic bonds. The chapter also covers the shapes of simple molecules and the VSEPR theory, hybridization of atomic orbitals and the molecular orbital theory of homonuclear diatomic molecules.

Chapter 5: States of Matter

Matter exists in three states - solid, liquid, and gas. This chapter covers the intermolecular forces that hold particles together in different states of matter, the characteristics of each state of matter, and the concept of the gas laws.

Chapter 6: Thermodynamics

Thermodynamics deals with the study of energy and its transformation. This chapter covers the various thermodynamic terms such as heat, work, internal energy, enthalpy, and the laws of thermodynamics. The chapter also covers the concept of entropy and the spontaneity of chemical reactions.

Chapter 7: Equilibrium

Chemical equilibrium is the state where the rate of the forward and reverse reactions is equal. This chapter covers the law of chemical equilibrium, the equilibrium constant, factors affecting equilibrium, the concept of Le Chatelier's principle, and the relationship between equilibrium constant and Gibbs energy change.

Chapter 8: Redox Reactions

Redox reactions involve the transfer of electrons from one species to another. This chapter covers the concept of oxidation and reduction, balancing redox reactions using oxidation number method and half-reaction method, electrochemical cells, and the Nernst equation.

Chapter 9: Hydrogen

Hydrogen is the first element in the periodic table. This chapter covers the position of hydrogen in the periodic table, its isotopes, preparation, properties, and uses of dihydrogen. The chapter also covers the concept of hydrogen bonding and its importance in biological systems.
