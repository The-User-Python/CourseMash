Principles of Accounting

  • Entity Concept: Business transactions must be separate from personal transactions.
  • Going Concern Concept: The assumption that a business will continue to operate in the foreseeable future.
  • Cost Concept: Assets are recorded at their original cost, not their current market value.
  • Consistency Concept: Accounting methods and procedures should be consistent over time.
  • Matching Concept: Expenses should be matched with the revenue they generate.
  • Materiality Concept: Only significant transactions need to be recorded.
  • Conservatism Concept: Record potential losses as soon as possible, but delay recording potential gains.
  • Full Disclosure Concept: All significant information about the business should be disclosed in the financial statements.
  • Time Period Concept: Financial statements should be prepared for a specific period of time, such as a year or a quarter.
  • Objectivity Concept: Accounting information should be free from bias and personal opinion.
