Introduction

Accountancy is the process of identifying, measuring, recording, and communicating financial information about an entity to interested parties.

Basic Accounting Terms

  • Assets: Resources owned by an entity that have monetary value and are expected to provide future economic benefits.
  • Liabilities: Obligations of an entity to transfer economic resources to other entities in the future as a result of past transactions or events.
  • Equity: The residual interest in the assets of an entity after deducting liabilities.
  • Revenues: Inflows of economic resources resulting from the sale of goods or services.
  • Expenses: Outflows of economic resources resulting from the consumption of goods or services in the process of earning revenue.

Financial Statements

Financial statements are reports that provide information about an entity's financial position, performance, and cash flows. The main financial statements are:

  • Balance Sheet: Provides information about an entity's assets, liabilities, and equity at a specific point in time.
