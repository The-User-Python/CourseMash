Basic Rules of Accounting

  • Business Entity Concept: The financial transactions of a business must be recorded separately from those of its owner(s) or any other business.
  • Going Concern Concept: The financial statements of a business are prepared on the assumption that the business will continue to operate indefinitely.
  • Money Measurement Concept: Only those transactions that can be expressed in monetary terms are recorded in the books of accounts.
  • Cost Concept: Assets are recorded at their original cost price and are not revalued in the books of accounts.
  • Duality Concept: Every transaction has two aspects - debit and credit, and the total of debits must be equal to the total of credits.
  • Accounting Period Concept: The financial statements of a business are prepared for a specific period of time, usually a year or a quarter.
  • Matching Concept: Revenues and expenses are recorded in the same accounting period in which they occur.
  • Conservatism Concept: When there is uncertainty about the value of an asset or liability, the accountant should record the lower value in the books of accounts.
  • Materiality Concept: Only significant transactions and events are recorded in the books of accounts.
  • Consistency Concept: Accounting policies and procedures should be applied consistently from one period to the next.
